About Us

Adventure Meets Tradition: Where Rapids Roar and Nature Soars

History

Redhawk Rapids' storied history, founded by Native American Aiyana Redhawk, blends thrilling river expeditions with an unwavering commitment to environmental preservation, creating a legacy of responsible adventure tourism spanning over a century.

Native American Legend

Founded by Native American Aiyana Redhawk in the early 1900s, "Redhawk Rapids" began as a Grand Canyon white water rafting company. Aiyana's deep connection to the land led to thrilling river expeditions. His commitment to environmental preservation set Redhawk Rapids apart. The company grew, becoming an icon of responsible adventure tourism. Aiyana's daughter, Sakari, continued his legacy, expanding operations across the American West. Today, Redhawk Rapids remains a symbol of Native American heritage and sustainable adventure, offering unforgettable journeys while safeguarding the natural beauty of the rivers and canyons that have been its home for over a century.

His Legacy

Aiyana Redhawk's passing in the mid-20th century marked a pivotal moment for Redhawk Rapids. His daughter, Sakari, stepped into leadership, preserving her father's commitment to environmental stewardship. Sakari expanded the company, embracing new territories and maintaining a strong focus on sustainable adventure. Redhawk Rapids became a beacon of responsible tourism, emphasizing conservation and education. The company formed the Redhawk Foundation to support preservation efforts. An annual event celebrated Aiyana's legacy at the Grand Canyon base camp, drawing adventurers and storytellers to honor his vision. Sakari's children, Rhiannon Aiyana, Elanor Aiyana continued the legacy, ensuring Redhawk Rapids remained a symbol of Native American heritage and environmental preservation.
